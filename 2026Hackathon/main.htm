What?

This year is the 25th anniversary of STIR and we are happy to announce a STIR hackathon in London, UK. This hackathon is intended for existing users of STIR who are interested in expanding their knowledge and even becoming STIR developers. We anticipate to cover the following themes:

  • Reconstructing measured PET and SPECT data with STIR
  • Using PET Monte Carlo software (GATE, SimSET, Simind) with STIR
  • Improving GPU performance

(For up-to-date information on this meeting please always check the STIR website.)

Where and when?

Location: National Physical Laboratory, Teddington, London, UK.
Dates: Thu 5 March - Fri 6 March 2026

Timing is chosen such that participants can afterwards join the Symposium on AI and Reconstruction for Biomedical Imaging (9-10 March) and the Hackathon on software for machine learning approaches in biomedical imaging (11-12 March)

The hackathon will be mainly in-person, but we will arrange hybrid access for access to the main talks, and try to enable online collaboration between remote participants.
